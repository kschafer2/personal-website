About Me

I was born and raised in Lee's Summit, Missouri as the second son of Steven and Suzanne Schafer. As a young child, I attended Hazel Grove Elementary and enjoyed my evenings drawing and playing basketball in the backyard with my older brother, Brian. After elementary school, my family and I moved to the other side of town, where I dove into the world of computer games and computer hardware. I built my first computer during middle school and continued building computers and gaming throughout high school.

Full of curiosity and thirsty for knowledge, I studied kinesiology at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas for one year. After deciding to attend college closer to home, I transferred to the University of Missouri, Columbia where my interests continued to broaden. Rock climbing became a staple of my lifestyle, and my love for science resulted in the acquisition of B.A. in Biology and B.S. in Psychology.

Ultimately, these degrees did not satisfy my cravings for learning and technology, so I decided to formally continue my education of computers at Columbia College. I am excited to graduate with a B.S. in Computer Science and continue to hone my software development skills and technical knowledge in the workforce.
